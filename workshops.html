#1: Neighborhood Flyer


Prompt: Design a neighbourhood digital flyer for an upcoming local event, fictional or real

.

Timeline

  1. Prompt (30 minutes)
  2. Gather Layers and Assemble (90 minutes)

    3. { 1 hour lunch break! }

  3. Layout & Scan (1.5 hour): You may use a variety of free and publicly accessible online tools from www.a-s-c.org.
  4. Print (1 hour): Together we will print each of your flyers on coloured paper. And I will email you a copy of the digital version. I will also add your digital version to a website page. Which you can add to your resume/CV!
  5. Celebrate! Discuss each other’s flyers.

Prompt

In a globalized world, being informed and inspired by your everyday life becomes ever important to be rooted in the here and now.

This workshop focuses on graphic design, typography, and do-it-yourself publishing. It will explore combining text and images to create custom designs to promote an event for a specific time and place.

Participants will be asked to contribute their flyers to an online gallery website.

You will understand the weight and potential of communicating with words and images in tandem. By understanding your message's clarity for a specific context, while embracing to add to your portfolio. Perhaps this could inspire you to hold space for local events!


Sizes and formats:

  • Print: 8.5 inches x 11 inches
  • Digital: 816 pixes x 1054 pixels

Choose a neighbourhood and jot down:

  • A: What does not exist locally in your neighbourhood that you’d love to have access to?
  • B: What exists locally in your neighbourhood that you’d like to promote?
  • Who is this for?
  • What is this about?
  • Where does it take place?
  • When does it happen?
  • How will people get there?
  • How long does it last?>
